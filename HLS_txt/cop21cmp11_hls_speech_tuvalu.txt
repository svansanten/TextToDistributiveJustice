      TUVALU  Keynote Statement Delivered by The Prime Minister of Tuvalu, the Honourable Enele S. Sopoaga At the High Level Segment of the COP21, 7th December 2015.	
  	
  	
  	
  	
  	
  	
  	
  	
  	
  	
  	
  
Mr	
  President,	
  Head	
  of	
  Government,	
  Ministers,	
  	
  Excellencies,	
  Ladies	
  and	
  Gentlemen,	
  	
  We	
  are	
  on	
  the	
  last	
  week	
  of	
  the	
  COP21	
  negotiations.	
  	
  We	
  stand	
  here	
  facing	
  one	
  of	
  the	
  greatest	
   challenges	
   of	
   humankind,	
   namely	
  climate	
   change.	
   We	
   have	
   no	
   time	
   left	
   for	
  rhetoric	
  or	
  hiding.	
  We	
  must	
  all	
  stand	
  together	
  and	
  face	
  up	
  to	
  our	
  responsibilities	
  and	
  a	
  finalise	
   a	
   legally	
   binding	
   agreement	
   that	
   will	
   deliver	
   a	
   temperature	
   stabilisation	
  pathway	
  below	
  1.5	
  degrees	
  Celsius	
  compared	
  with	
  pre-­‐industrial	
  levels.	
  	
  	
  The	
   Science	
   is	
  clear	
   and	
  indisputable;	
   human	
   induced	
   climatic	
   impacts	
   are	
   already	
  occurring	
   at	
   the	
   current	
   level	
   of	
   global	
   warming	
   and	
   will	
   only	
  accelerate	
  the	
   risk	
   of	
  severe,	
  pervasive	
  and	
  irreversible	
  impacts.	
  	
  At	
  current	
  warming,	
  Tuvalu’s	
  future	
  is	
  already	
  bleak.	
  We	
  must	
  urgently	
  cut	
  greenhouse	
  gases,	
  and	
  dramatically	
  transform	
  the	
  global	
  economy	
  to	
  a	
  renewable	
  energy	
  pathway.	
  Any	
  further	
  temperature	
  increase	
  beyond	
  1.5	
  °C	
  will	
  spell	
  the	
  total	
  demise	
  of	
  Tuvalu,	
  and	
  other	
  low	
  lying	
  island	
  nations.	
  	
  	
  We	
  must	
  have	
  the	
  greatest	
  possible	
  political	
  will	
  to	
  set	
  the	
  right	
  path	
  here	
  in	
  Paris	
  that	
  will	
  “leave	
  no	
  one	
  behind”.	
  	
  We	
  must	
  agree	
  to	
  a	
  Paris	
  Agreement	
  that	
  is	
  legally	
  binding.	
  	
  Anything	
  less	
  would	
  signal	
  to	
  the	
  world	
  that	
  we	
  are	
  not	
  serious	
  about	
  climate	
  change	
  and	
  that	
  will	
  be	
  morally	
  shameful.	
  	
  	
  	
  Having	
   committed	
   our	
  INDCs,	
   we	
   must	
   obliged	
   to	
  action	
   and	
  implement	
  each	
   of	
   our	
  respective.	
  This	
  cannot	
  be	
  a	
  communication	
  agreement,	
  it	
  must	
  be	
  an	
  implementation	
  agreement.	
  We	
  must	
  have	
  a	
  five	
  year	
  review	
  cycle,	
  to	
  gauge	
  and	
  increase	
  our	
  ambition	
  to	
  a	
  desired	
  commitment	
  and	
  target	
  for	
  all	
  Parties.	
  	
  	
  We	
  need	
  a	
  permanent	
  mechanism	
  for	
  Loss	
  and	
  Damage	
  anchored	
  in	
  the	
  Paris	
  Treaty	
  to	
  give	
   us	
   the	
   assurance	
   that	
   the	
   necessary	
   response	
   to	
   climate	
   change	
   impacts	
   will	
   be	
  forthcoming.	
  This	
   is	
   important	
   to	
   Tuvalu	
  and	
   other	
   low-­‐lying	
   small	
   island	
   developing	
  states	
  to	
  safeguard	
  their	
  survival	
  from	
  increased	
  sea	
  level,	
  and	
  other	
  severe	
  impacts	
  of	
  climate	
  change.	
  	
  The	
  Paris	
  treaty	
  must	
  also	
  deliver,	
  new	
  and	
  predictable	
  finance,	
  technology	
  transfer	
  and	
  capacity	
  building.	
  	
  With	
  respect	
  to	
  finance	
  we	
  must	
  be	
  assured	
  that	
  the	
  most	
  vulnerable	
  are	
  supported	
  financially	
  in	
  their	
  mitigation	
  and	
  resilience-­‐building	
  endeavours.	
  	
  Easy	
  access	
  and	
  streamlined	
  processes	
  must	
  be	
  established	
  for	
  these	
  financial	
  vehicles	
  like	
  the	
  Green	
  Climate	
  Fund	
  and	
  GEF.	
  	
  We	
  must	
  also	
  ensure	
  that	
  the	
  financial	
  partnerships	
  are	
  scheduled	
  for	
  delivery	
  in	
  a	
  timely	
  manner.	
  	
  	
  	
  	
  Mr.	
  President,	
  	
  The	
  Paris	
  treaty	
  must	
  have	
  clear	
  objectives	
  that	
  protect	
  human	
  rights,	
  it	
  must	
  promote	
  gender	
  equality,	
  respect	
  the	
  rights	
  of	
  Indigenous	
  Peoples	
  and	
  and	
  ensure	
  that	
  all	
  actions	
  under	
   the	
   treaty	
   are	
   environmentally	
   sound	
   and	
   do	
   not	
   adversely	
   affect	
   the	
   natural	
  ecosystems.	
  	
  Mr.	
  President	
  Time	
  is	
  of	
  essence.	
  We	
  must	
  all	
  stand	
  together	
  to	
  save	
  our	
  future	
  and	
  our	
  one	
  planet	
  earth.A	
  new,	
  comprehensive	
  treaty	
  on	
  climate	
  change	
  is	
  doable.	
  Lets	
  do	
  it	
  now.	
  Let’s	
  do	
  it	
  for	
  the	
  future	
  of	
  humanity	
  and	
  all	
  lives	
  on	
  this	
  planet.	
  	
  	
  Let’s	
  do	
  it	
  for	
  Tuvalu.	
  For	
  if	
  we	
  save	
  Tuvalu	
  we	
  save	
  the	
  world.	
  	
  I	
  thank	
  you.
